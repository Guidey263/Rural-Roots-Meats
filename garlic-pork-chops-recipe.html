Garlic Herb Pan-Seared Pork Chops

Recipe by Rural Roots Meats

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in or boneless pork chops (about 1 inch thick)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional garnish: fresh parsley or more thyme for serving

Instructions:

  1. **Prep the Pork Chops:** Pat the pork chops completely dry with paper towels. Season both sides generously with salt and black pepper. Let the chops sit at room temperature for about 10-15 minutes.
  2. **Heat the Pan:** Place a heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron works best) over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil to the pan.
  3. **Sear the Pork Chops:** Carefully place the pork chops in the hot skillet. Sear for 3-4 minutes per side, or until a deep golden-brown crust has formed.
  4. **Add Butter, Garlic, and Herbs:** Reduce the heat to medium. Add the butter, minced garlic, rosemary, and thyme to the pan. Baste the pork chops with the fragrant butter mixture for about 1-2 minutes.
  5. **Finish Cooking:** Continue to cook for another 2-4 minutes, or until a thermometer registers **145�F (63�C)** in the thickest part of the chop.
  6. **Rest and Serve:** Remove the pork chops from the pan and let them rest for at least 5 minutes before serving. Serve with the pan juices.
